Your contribution to the environment begins with knowledge. We have gathered the most important information to make the process of sorting and dropping off bottles as simple and meaningful as possible.

1. Which plastic can and cannot be dropped off? (Marking) üß¥

For the Eco-QR project, bottles marked PET (1) are the most important. Knowing all types helps you better understand what goes into recycling and what doesn't.

1 - PET

Water bottles, juices, sodas, vegetable oils. The most common and easily recyclable type. Accepted by Eco-QR.

2 - PEHD (HDPE)

High-density polyethylene. Milk containers, shampoo, soap, caps. Safe and well-recycled. Sometimes accepted at specialized points.

3 - PVC

Window/floor coverings, oilcloths. Considered hazardous, difficult to recycle (releases toxins when burned). Not accepted.

4 - LDPE

Bags, cling film, garbage bags. Low-density polyethylene. Accepted for recycling without problems.

5 - PP

Yogurt cups, toys, disposable tableware. Heat-resistant and durable. Recycling is more difficult, accepted less frequently than PET and PEHD.

6 - PS

Foamed plastic (styrofoam), meat trays, vegetable boxes. Contains styrene. Not accepted everywhere, best to avoid.

7 - OTHER

Mixes of plastic and other types. Usually hard containers.Not recyclable.

2. Why is it important to drop off plastic for recycling? üåç

Your action is not just a bonus, it is a contribution to the planet's health and the sustainability of Kazakhstan's economy.

Pollution Reduction

Plastic decomposes for hundreds of years and fills landfills. Recycling turns it into a valuable resource, significantly reducing the load on ecosystems.

Resource Saving

Recycling reduces the need for primary raw materials (oil and gas), conserving natural resources and reducing energy consumption during production (up to 37% less carbon footprint).

Economic Benefit

Recycled plastic (secondary polyester, granules) is used to manufacture furniture, clothing, and construction materials, creating new jobs and supporting the circular economy.

3. What happens to the bottle after you drop it off? üîÑ

1. Collection and Sorting

Bottles are collected at points, delivered to factories, and sorted by plastic type (PET, PEHD) and color (colorless, green, brown). Important: labels and caps are separated.

2. Cleaning and Grinding

Plastic is washed from residual contents, thoroughly cleaned, and dried. It is then shredded into small pieces called PET-flex (plastic flakes).

3. Granulation and Extrusion

PET-flex is melted and passed through an extruder, forming clean, reusable granules. This is the purest secondary raw material.

4. Second Life

New products are manufactured from the granules. Your old bottles can turn into:

  • New plastic bottles and containers.
  • Synthetic fiber (polyester fiber for clothing, fillers, insulation).
  • Furniture and construction materials.
